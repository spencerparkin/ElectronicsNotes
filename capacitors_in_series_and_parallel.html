Capacitors in Series and Parallel

In both cases, suppose a battery, a resistor, and then a network of capacitors are our circuit. Let the battery have constant voltage \(V_{bat}\), and the capacitors each have voltage \(V_i\), current \(I_i\), and capacitance \(C_i\). We'll be interested to find \(C_{eqv}\).

The Series Case

In this case, we have, for all \(i\), \(I=I_i\), and we have \(V_{eqv}=\sum_i V_i\), since \(V_{bat}=V_R+V_{eqv}\) and \(V_{bat}=V_R+\sum_i V_i\). We now recall that... $$ \frac{d}{dt}V_{eqv} = \frac{I}{C_{eqv}}, $$ ...and similarly... $$ \frac{d}{dt}V_i = \frac{I_i}{C_i}. $$ We then write... $$ \frac{I}{C_{eqv}}=\frac{d}{dt}V_{eqv}=\frac{d}{dt}\sum_iV_i = \sum_i\frac{d}{dt}V_i=\sum_i\frac{I_i}{C_i}=I\sum_i\frac{1}{C_i}. $$ Now since \(I\neq 0\) for all \(t\), we can divide through by it to get our result... $$ \frac{1}{C_{eqv}}=\sum_i\frac{1}{C_i}. $$

The Parallel Case

In this case, we have, for all \(i\), \(V_{eqv}=V_i\), since \(V_{bat}=V_R+V_{eqv}\) and \(V_{bat}=V_R+V_i\). And we have \(I=\sum_i I_i\). Recalling our relationships between current and voltage for each capacitor and the equivilant capacitor, we then write... $$ \frac{d}{dt}V_{eqv}=\frac{I}{C_{eqv}}=\frac{1}{C_{eqv}}\sum_iI_i=\frac{1}{C_{eqv}}\sum_iC_i\frac{d}{dt}V_i. $$ Using \(V_i=V_{eqv}\), this can be rearranged as... $$ \left(1-\frac{1}{C_{eqv}}\sum_i C_i\right)\frac{d}{dt}V_{eqv}=0. $$ But again, \(I\neq 0\) for all \(t\), so we get our result... $$ C_{eqv}=\sum_i C_i. $$
