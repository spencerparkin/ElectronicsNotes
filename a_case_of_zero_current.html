A Case of Zero Current

Consider the following circuit of resistors, all of which are of value \(R\).

Interestingly, from our model of electric current (as marbles on tracks), it is immediately clear that there is no current from junction \(B\) to \(C\) or vice-versa. In fact, as pointed out by Mark, by symmetry, it is contradictory to claim a direction of current flow between either of these two points. Anyhow, here we're going to show mathematically that \(I_{BC}=0\).

Note that in our notation, \(V_{AB}=V_{A} - V_{B}\), meaning the voltage across \(A\)-to-\(B\) is the voltage \(V_{A}\) at \(A\) differing from that \(V_{B}\) at \(B\).

Examining junction \(C\), Kirchhoff tells us that \(I_{AC}+I_{BC}=I_{CD}\). Likewise, examining junction \(B\), we find that \(I_{AB}=I_{BC}+I_{BD}\). Now see that... $$ \begin{align} I_{BC} &= I_{CD} - I_{AC} \\ &= V_{CD}/R - V_{AC}/R \\ &= (V_{CD} - V_{AC})/R \\ &= (V_C - V_D - V_A + V_C)/R \\ &= (2V_C - V_{bat})/R, \end{align} $$ ...since \(V_A=V_{bat}\) and \(V_D=0\). We now write... $$ \begin{align} RI_{AC} + RI_{BC} &= RI_{CD} \\ V_A - V_C + V_B - V_C &= V_C - V_D \\ V_B &= 3V_C - V_{bat}, \end{align} $$ ...and we write... $$ \begin{align} RI_{AB} &= RI_{BC} + RI_{BD} \\ V_A - V_B &= V_B - V_C + V_B - V_D \\ V_C &= 3V_B - V_{bat}. \end{align} $$ Combining these last two equations, we find that \(V_C = V_{bat}/2\) which, when plugged into our earlier equation for \(I_{BC}\), gives us the desired result.

Note that in general, finding the equivilant resistance of a sub-network of resistors will all boil down in the end to solving a system of linear equations. The task involves the application of Kirchhoff's Laws. Not all sub-networks can be tackled using what we know of resistors in series and parallel alone.
